Thrissur

The name Thrissur is a shortened form of the Tamil word Thiru-Shiva-Per-Ur literally translates to 'A Big City or Town with the three places of worship of the "Lord Shiva"'. The name owes itself to the most prominent feature of the city, that is the Vadakkumnathan Temple, which has Shiva as its presiding deity.[7]
